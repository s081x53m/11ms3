Comprehension<br>
Answer these questions.<br>
a. How is Willie Chandran different from the rest of his family?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Willie Chandran is different from the rest of his family as he is insecure about himself and his own identity.<br>
 <br>
b. Who is the main character of Half A Life? How is he described?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Willie Chandran is the main character of Half A Life. He was born in India 40-some years ago. His father is a Brahmin and mother a Dalit. His middle name comes from the name of an English writer called Somerest Maugham.<br>
 <br>
c. Why does Willie leave India?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Willie leaves India because he manages to get a scholarship to a college in London.<br>
 <br>
d. What is the revelation that Willie begin to feel in college and in London?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Willie began to understand that he was free to present himself as he wished. He could write his own revolution. He could remake himself, his past and his ancestry.<br>
 <br>
e. Why does Willie accompany Ana?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Willie accompanies Ana because she has a kind of high status in her colonial world and he can also find a complete acceptance there.<br>
 <br>
f. What is the central issue Naipal has raised in the novel?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
In the novel Naipal has raised the issue of cultural and racial identity.<br>

Grammar<br>
Change the following into indirect speech.<br>
a. She said, &quot;While I was having dinner, the phone rang.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
She said that while she was having dinner, the phone rang.<br>
(Simple Past and Past Progressive in time clauses do not normally change.)<br>
 <br>
 <br>
b. My friend said, &quot;Where are they staying?&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
My friend asked where they were staying.<br>
 <br>
c. Jamila said, &quot;I travel a lot in my job.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Jamila said that she travelled a lot in her job.<br>
 <br>
d. She said to me, &quot;We lived in China for five years.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
She told me that they had lived in China for five years.<br>
 <br>
e. He said to me, &quot;Do you like ice-cream?&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
He told me if I had liked ice-cream.<br>
 <br>
f. They said, &quot;Hurray! We've won the match.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
They exclaimed with delight that they had won the match.<br>
 <br>
g. He said, &quot;I'd tried everything without success, but this new medicine is great.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
He said that he'd tried everything without success, but this new medicine was great.<br>
 <br>
h. Sony said, &quot;I go to the gym next to your house.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Sony said that she went to the gym next to my house.<br>
 <br>
i. He said, &quot;Be quiet after 10 o'clock.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
He told me to be quiet after 10 o'clock.<br>
 <br>
j. He said, &quot;I don't want to go to the party unless he invites me.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
He said that he didn't want to go to the party unless he invited him.<br>
 <br>
k. He said to me, &quot;I will see you tomorrow if you meet me.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
He told me that he would see me the following day if I met him.<br>
 <br>
l. She said, &quot;If I were you, I would give up the work.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
She said that if she were me, she would give up the work.<br>
Simple Past and Past Continuous do not normally change in sentences with if (imaginary condition).<br>
