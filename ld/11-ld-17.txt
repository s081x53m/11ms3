Comprehension<br>
Answer the following questions.<br>
a. Why did millions of Palestinians leave their country?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Millions of Palestinians left their country because in their country, they were denied basic human rights to find a better life and better economic opportunities.<br>
 <br>
b. How is the global culture formed? Is cultural identity possible even in the global culture? Justify.<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
The global culture is formed by the youthful generations who have grown up in the midst of massive technological advancements. Even in a global society, cultural identity is conceivable. Social media, street journalism, and global transnational businesses all play a role in catalysing new global culture formation processes. Globalization fosters cultural identity by creating a feeling of belonging. People are becoming increasingly worried about the uniqueness and particularity of their own culture in the current age of globalisation. Cultural identity gives local knowledge worldwide importance as well as a feeling of self, community, and country.<br>
 <br>
c. Why can moving from one side of the world to the other be an economic challenge for newcomers?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Moving from one side of the world to the other can be an economic challenge for newcomers because integration programme needs time; starting from learning the language, integrating culturally, integration into the labour market.<br>
 <br>
d. How can immigrants foster economy at different magnitudes of scale in their new homes?<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
Immigrants ca foster economy at different magnitudes of scale in their new homes by starting their own businesses.<br>
 <br>
e. Can immigrants fully integrate in the new societies? Give reasons.<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
No, immigrants can't fully integrate in the new societies because they are unwilling to be part of the new society and the society does not open its doors fully to allow them to integrate.<br>
 <br>
f. Mention any two benefits of integrating into a new society for the immigrants.<br>
 <br>
Answer:<br>
- Migrants will have equal learning, engagement, and self-esteem possibilities. After that, they may serve as a strong bridge for mutual understanding between the two cultures or faiths.<br>
- Wherever immigrants come from, the receiving nations will improve their economic prospects.<br>

 <br>

- They can face the fear of losing identity.<br>
